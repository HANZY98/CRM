English Proficiency Verification

First check that the applicant’s identification document does not state they were born in one of the following English-speaking nations:

  • United Kingdom.
  • Republic of Ireland.
  • Any British Constituency or Crown Dependency.
  • United States of America.
  • Canada.
  • Australia.
  • New Zealand.

Then check that the applicant’s most recent 2 years of education were not completed in an English-speaking nation by checking the location and date of their most recent education qualification.

Then check that the applicant has submitted one of the following documents to verify their English proficiency:

  • Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) B2 certificate.
  • IELTS 5.5 certificate, including 5.5 for reading and writing.
  • PTE Academic 51 or equivalent certificate.

Finally, check that the applicant’s English proficiency qualification certificate belongs to the applicant; i.e. check the name and any other cross-referenceable information.
